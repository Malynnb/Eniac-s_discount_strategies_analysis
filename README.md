# Eniac's_discount_strategies_analysis
This project analyses how discount strategies impact revenue, order volume, and product performance in an e-commerce dataset.  The goal is to understand whether discounts truly drive sales or reduce profitability.


# Key Questions
How much are products discounted on average?
Do higher discounts lead to higher revenue?
Which products and categories benefit most from discounts?
Are there optimal discount ranges?

# Data Cleaning
Removed missing and inconsistent price values
Converted date columns to datetime format
Handled outliers (extreme price differences)
Ensured consistency between product and order data

# Feature Engineering
Created:
discount
discount_pct
Categorised discounts into tiers:
Low (<10%)
Medium (10–25%)
High (>25%)

# Key Insights
Most products are discounted, but not all discounts increase revenue
Mid-range discounts often perform better than extreme discounts
Some products generate high revenue without heavy discounts
Discount strategy effectiveness varies across product categories
